How to format Environment Agency data

Use these date and time standards for consistency and to reduce errors and ambiguity in your Environment Agency data returns.

Character definitions

Use the following character definitions in this service:

  • [Y] represents a digit used in the time element year
  • [M] represents a digit used in the time element month
  • [D] represents a digit used in the time element day
  • [h] represents a digit used in the time element hour
  • [m] represents a digit used in the time element minute
  • [s] represents a digit used in the time element second
  • T represents the start of the time component of a recorded date and time

Date format

Record a date as YYYY-MM-DD or DD-MM-YYYY where:

  • [YYYY] is the full 4-digit year
  • [MM] is the 2-digit calendar month number from 01 to 12, with a leading 0 inserted for the months of January to September, eg 01 for January
  • [DD] is the calendar day from 01 to 31 (01 to 30 for April, June, September and November and 01 to 28 for February with 29 in a leap year) with a leading 0 inserted for days before the 10th day of a month, eg 02 for 2 January
  • the separator character is a hyphen '-'
  • eg 3 September 1972 is recorded as 1972-09-03

Time format

Record a time as hh:mm:ss where:

  • [hh] is the full 2-digit hour using the 24-hour clock that runs from 00 to 23 with a leading 0 inserted for the hours before 10am, eg 07 for 7am
  • [mm] is the full 2-digit minute that runs from 00 to 60 with a leading 0 inserted for the minutes before 10 minutes, eg 06 for 6 minutes
  • [ss] is the full 2-digit second that runs from 00 to 60, with a leading 0 inserted for the seconds before 10 seconds, eg 02 for 2 seconds
  • the separator character is a colon ':'
  • examples: 4 seconds past 7:13am is recorded as 07:13:04; 10 seconds past 11:04pm is recorded as 23:04:10

Date and time format

Record date and time together as YYYY-MM-DDThh:mm:ss or DD-MM-YYYYThh:mm:ss where:

  • date is recorded as above
  • time is recorded as above
  • a T is inserted between the date and time
  • examples: 4 seconds past 7:13am on 3 September 1972 is recorded as 1972-09-02T07:13:04; 10 seconds past 11:04pm on 16 December 2013 is recorded as 2013-12-16T23:04:10

Reduced precision formats

If you don't have to give a precise date and time (or it's not actually known), you can amend the full date and time format by omitting digits and characters, starting from the extreme right-hand side.

This has the effect of reducing the precision of the recorded date and time.

Examples:

Situation Action Example
If you need the exact date and time to the nearest second Record full date and time 2013-09-03T12:01:56
If you need the date and time to the nearest minute Omit seconds from the time element 2013-09-03T12:01
If you need the date and time to the nearest hour Omit seconds from the time element 2013-09-03T12
If you need the date to the nearest day Omit the time element 2013-09-03
If you need the date to the nearest month Omit the day and time elements 2013-09
If you need the year Omit the month, day and time elements 2013

Date and time range

Use the format above for the time of the start interval and again for the end interval with a forward slash / as a separator character. Eg YYYY-MM-DDThh:mm:ss/YYYY-MM-DDThh:mm:ss

You can also use reduced precision formats in date and time ranges.

Examples:

Situation Action Example
If you need the exact date and time range to the nearest second Record full dates and times with the slash separator 2013-09-03T12:01:56/2014-09-04T12:30:04
If you need the date and time range to the nearest minute Omit seconds from the time element 2013-09-03T12:01/2014-09-04T12:30
If you need the date range to the nearest day Omit the time element 2013-09-03/2014-09-04
If you need the date range to the nearest month Omit the day and time elements 2013-09/2014-09
If you need the range from year to year Omit the day, month and time elements 2013/2014

Date range terms

Date range terms and their meanings are as follows:

Term Meaning
Quarter 1 Jan-Mar YYYY
Quarter 2 Apr-Jun YYYY
Quarter 3 Jul-Sep YYYY
Quarter 4 Oct-Dec YYYY
First half of the year Jan-Jun YYYY
Second half of the year Jul-Dec YYYY
Calendar year YYYY
Financial year YY/YY or YYYY/YY

Date and time in IT software

Don't store date and time data in databases as strings or digits but in the pre-defined date and time format of your database software.

Client software interfacing with the database must display and transfer date and time data in a way that's consistent with this service.
