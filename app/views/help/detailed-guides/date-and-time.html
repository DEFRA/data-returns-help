How to format Environment Agency data

Use these date and time standards for consistency and to reduce errors and ambiguity in your Environment Agency data returns.

Character definitions

Use the following character definitions in this service:

  • [Y] represents a digit used in the time element year
  • [M] represents a digit used in the time element month
  • [D] represents a digit used in the time element day
  • [h] represents a digit used in the time element hour
  • [m] represents a digit used in the time element minute
  • [s] represents a digit used in the time element second
  • T represents the start of the time component of a recorded date and time

Time format

Record a time as hh:mm:ss where:

  • [hh] is the full 2-digit hour using the 24-hour clock that runs from 00 to 23 with a leading 0 inserted for the hours before 10am, eg 07 for 7am
  • [mm] is the full 2-digit minute that runs from 00 to 60 with a leading 0 inserted for the minutes before 10 minutes, eg 06 for 6 minutes
  • [ss] is the full 2-digit second that runs from 00 to 60, with a leading 0 inserted for the seconds before 10 seconds, eg 02 for 2 seconds
  • the separator character is a colon ':'
  • examples: 4 seconds past 7:13am is recorded as 07:13:04; 10 seconds past 11:04pm is recorded as 23:04:10

Date and time together format

Record date and time together as YYYY-MM-DDThh:mm:ss or DD-MM-YYYYThh:mm:ss where:

  • date is recorded as above
  • time is recorded as above
  • a T is inserted between the date and time
  • examples: 4 seconds past 7:13am on 3 September 1972 can be recorded as 1972-09-02T07:13:04; 10 seconds past 11:04pm on 16 December 2013 can be recorded as 2013-12-16T23:04:10

Date and time range

Use the format above for the time of the start interval and again for the end interval with a forward slash / as a separator character. Eg YYYY-MM-DDThh:mm:ss/YYYY-MM-DDThh:mm:ss

Date range terms

Date range terms and their meanings are as follows:

Term Meaning
Quarter 1 Jan-Mar YYYY
Quarter 2 Apr-Jun YYYY
Quarter 3 Jul-Sep YYYY
Quarter 4 Oct-Dec YYYY
First half of the year Jan-Jun YYYY
Second half of the year Jul-Dec YYYY
Calendar year YYYY
Financial year YY/YY or YYYY/YY

Date and time in IT software

Don't store date and time data in databases as strings or digits but in the pre-defined date and time format of your database software.

Client software interfacing with the database must display and transfer date and time data in a way that's consistent with this service.

Documents

BS ISO 8601:2004 - Representation of dates and times. See the DMMI National Lead for Data Standards for this external standard if you require it.
